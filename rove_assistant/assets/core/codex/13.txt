That Slumbrous Influence Kest
Makaal’s jubilant clapping echoes around the wooden enclosure. “I honestly thought that coffer was lost for good! Help yourself to the stores inside; I was expecting to leave it behind when we escaped. Not only would I have never guessed the nektari took off with the key, but you could not pay me to try and wrest anything from one of their hives.”
The merchant’s willingness to part with so much of his supply seems odd, but perhaps he truly had lost hope of leaving this place alive. His hurried whispers and gestures to placate his starling partner are suspicious, but his hushed reasoning seems to pass muster as the starling’s posture relaxes after their quiet discussion.
